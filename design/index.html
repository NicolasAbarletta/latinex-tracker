BGFG
Banco General, S.A.
🇵🇦 Banks · Panama · $5,500M market cap · listed since 1972
★ Quality compounder

Banco General is the most profitable, best-capitalized bank on Latinex, compounding a 16.8% ROE while running less leverage than its peers (9.9x vs 10.2x median). The story is consistency, not growth: earnings rose ~17% over FY2023→FY2025 on a wide deposit franchise and best-in-class efficiency (44% cost/income). The catch is valuation — at 12.4x earnings and 2.05x book it is priced for perfection, and the net margin trails specialized peers because of its universal-bank mix.

What's working

strengths
  • ✓
    High-quality ROE. 16.8% return on 9.9x leverage — earned through margins and efficiency, not balance-sheet risk.
  • ✓
    Best-in-class efficiency. 44% cost/income vs 49% peer median — every revenue dollar drops more to the bottom line.
  • ✓
    Fortress capital. Equity/assets of 10.1% gives a thick buffer and room to keep paying dividends through a cycle.
  • ✓
    Wide, cheap funding. Dominant retail deposit base supports a 3.4% NIM, above the 3.1% peer median.
  • ✓
    Reliable capital return. Consistent dividend, 4.1% yield, with a conservative payout that looks sustainable.

What needs watching

risks & gaps
  • !
    Premium valuation. 12.4x P/E and 2.05x P/B leave little margin of safety if growth or rates disappoint.
  • !
    Thinner net margin. 28% vs 35.5% peer median — the universal-bank cost base dilutes profitability per dollar of revenue.
  • !
    Yield below peers. 4.1% dividend yield trails the 5.4% group median — income investors are paid less to wait.
  • !
    Moderating growth. Earnings growth is decelerating; the franchise is mature in a small domestic market.
  • !
    Local liquidity. Limited float on Latinex makes building or exiting a position slow versus the listed peers.
